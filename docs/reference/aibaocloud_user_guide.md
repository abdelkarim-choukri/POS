# 爱宝美食云 — User Capability & Feature Guide
> What the platform does, who uses it, and why it matters — explained in plain language.
> Organized by user role and functional area.

---

## Who Uses This Platform?

The platform serves several distinct types of users, each with different needs:

| User Type | Who They Are |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Store Owner / Admin** | Owns or manages one or more restaurant/food service locations. Full control over settings, staff, products, and data. |
| **Store Manager** | Day-to-day operator of a single store. Manages products, tables, customers, and runs reports. |
| **Franchise / Chain Manager** | Oversees multiple stores under one brand. Coordinates goods, promotions, and performance across locations. |
| **Marketing Staff** | Responsible for customer loyalty, promotions, coupons, and campaigns. |
| **Warehouse / Purchasing Staff** | Handles stock levels, purchase orders, and vendor relationships. |
| **Customer** | End consumer who interacts with loyalty programs, coupons, and membership features. |
| **System Administrator** | Platform-level admin managing accounts, regions, and platform-wide settings. |

# SECTION 1 — GETTING INTO THE SYSTEM
### (Authentication & Account Management)

---

## 1.1 Logging In

**What it does:**
Lets any registered user securely sign into the platform with their username and password.

**Who uses it:**
Every user — from store owners to warehouse staff — before they can do anything.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Access their personalised dashboard and tools
- Pick which store to operate (if they manage multiple locations)
- Resume exactly where they left off

**Why it matters:**
This is the front door to the entire system. Without it, nothing else is accessible. The login is designed to be fast so staff aren't kept waiting at shift start.

---

## 1.2 Choosing Your Store at Login

**What it does:**
After entering credentials, users who manage more than one location see a list of their stores and choose which one to work in for that session.

**Who uses it:**
Franchise owners, regional managers, or any staff member who has access to more than one location.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Switch between stores without logging out
- See only the stores they're permitted to manage
- Access a "proxy" store view for franchise oversight

**Why it matters:**
A manager overseeing three locations doesn't need three separate logins. They pick the store they need today, and everything — products, customers, reports — is scoped to that location.

---

## 1.3 Registering a New Account

**What it does:**
Allows new business owners or operators to create an account on the platform.

**Who uses it:**
New merchants signing up to use AiBao Cloud for their restaurant or food service business.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Create a personal account with username, password, and contact details
- Verify their identity via SMS or email
- Choose their service region and agree to terms

**Why it matters:**
This is how new customers join the platform. A smooth registration experience reduces drop-off and gets new businesses onboarded quickly.

---

## 1.4 Verifying Your Identity (SMS & Email Codes)

**What it does:**
Sends a one-time code to a phone number or email address to confirm the user is who they say they are.

**Who uses it:**
Any user during registration, password reset, or sensitive account changes.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Confirm their phone number or email is valid and accessible
- Complete two-step verification for security-sensitive actions

**Why it matters:**
Protects user accounts from unauthorised access. Even if someone guesses a password, they can't complete critical actions without also having access to the registered phone or email.

---

## 1.5 Resetting a Forgotten Password

**What it does:**
Lets users who have forgotten their password regain access to their account through verified contact details.

**Who uses it:**
Any user who is locked out of their account.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Request a recovery code via SMS or email
- Verify their identity
- Set a brand new password and regain full access

**Why it matters:**
Without this, a forgotten password would mean calling support and potentially losing hours of work time. Self-service recovery keeps operations running smoothly.

---

## 1.6 Editing Your Profile

**What it does:**
Allows users to update their own account information, such as their display name, contact details, or other personal settings.

**Who uses it:**
Any logged-in user managing their own account.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Update their name or contact information
- Keep their profile current as their role or details change

**Why it matters:**
Accurate user profiles ensure the right people receive notifications, and that records (like "created by") in the system are attributable to the correct person.

---

## 1.7 Logging Out

**What it does:**
Ends the current user session and securely signs the user out.

**Who uses it:**
Anyone using a shared device or finishing their shift.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Securely end their session on shared terminals
- Prevent unauthorised access after they walk away

**Why it matters:**
In a restaurant environment where multiple staff share a single device, this is critical. It prevents one staff member from accidentally (or intentionally) taking actions under another person's account.

---

## 1.8 Viewing the User List

**What it does:**
Shows administrators a paginated list of all user accounts associated with their store or organisation.

**Who uses it:**
Store owners and system administrators.

**What they can accomplish:**
- See who has access to the system
- Review staff accounts
- Identify unused or unnecessary accounts

**Why it matters:**
Gives owners visibility and control over who can access their business data. Useful for auditing access during staff turnover.

---

## 1.9 Checking Version History / Changelogs

**What it does:**
Displays a log of platform updates, showing what new features or fixes have been released.

**Who uses it:**
Store owners, managers, or any user wanting to understand what has changed in the platform.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Browse a menu of release categories
- Read detailed notes about what changed in each version
- Stay informed about new capabilities they can use

**Why it matters:**
Keeps users informed so they can take advantage of new features and understand why something might work differently after an update. Reduces confusion and support requests.

# SECTION 2 — MANAGING YOUR STORE
### (Store Configuration & Settings)

---

## 2.1 Viewing Store Information

**What it does:**
Retrieves the full profile and configuration of a specific store location.

**Who uses it:**
Store owners and managers when they need to review or verify store details.

**What they can accomplish:**
- See the store's registered name, code, and configuration
- Confirm store details before making changes
- Reference store data when contacting support

**Why it matters:**
Having a single, accurate source of truth for store information prevents errors in operations, billing, and reporting.

---

## 2.2 Store Settings & Parameters

**What it does:**
Allows store administrators to configure how the store operates — things like settlement times, product batch management, and operational toggles.

**Who uses it:**
Store owners and senior managers customising the system to fit their specific workflow.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Set the daily settlement cutoff time (e.g., when the business day "resets")
- Toggle features like batch product management on or off
- Save a customised configuration that persists across sessions

**Why it matters:**
Every restaurant operates differently. A 24-hour diner needs different settings than a lunch-only café. These settings let the platform adapt to how the business actually works, rather than forcing a one-size-fits-all approach.

---

## 2.3 Restoring Default Settings

**What it does:**
Resets store parameters back to the platform's original default values.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers who have misconfigured something and want to start fresh.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Undo all custom configuration changes at once
- Return to a known-good baseline if settings are causing problems

**Why it matters:**
Misconfigured settings can cause operational chaos — wrong settlement times, missing features, broken workflows. A one-click reset is a safety net that prevents small mistakes from becoming big problems.

---

## 2.4 Checking SMS Message Balance

**What it does:**
Shows how many SMS messages the store has remaining in their messaging allowance.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers and marketing staff before launching customer notification campaigns.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Know in advance if they have enough messages for a planned campaign
- Avoid sending failures mid-campaign due to running out of balance
- Decide whether to top up before sending

**Why it matters:**
Nothing damages a marketing campaign more than messages failing to send halfway through. Checking the balance first prevents surprise failures at critical moments.

---

## 2.5 Reading Store Announcements & Notices

**What it does:**
Displays published messages and homepage notices from the platform operator to store users.

**Who uses it:**
All store staff when they log in or check the dashboard.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Stay informed about platform maintenance windows
- Receive important operational notices
- Learn about new features or required actions

**Why it matters:**
Critical communications (like "the system will be down for maintenance Saturday night") need to reach every user reliably. These notices ensure no one is caught off guard.

---

## 2.6 Checking for Expiring Products

**What it does:**
Alerts staff to products in the system that are approaching their expiration date.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers and kitchen staff as part of daily inventory checks.

**What they can accomplish:**
- See a list of products with upcoming expiry dates
- Take action before items expire (use them first, apply discounts, remove from menu)
- Avoid serving expired products to customers

**Why it matters:**
Food safety and waste reduction are critical in food service. Catching expiring items before it's too late can save significant money and protect customer health.

---

## 2.7 Registering with FCDR (Financial Compliance)

**What it does:**
Connects the store to the FCDR financial compliance system and generates the required QR code.

**Who uses it:**
Store owners during initial setup or when meeting new regulatory requirements.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Complete the financial registration process required for compliant operation
- Receive their official FCDR QR code for display at the store

**Why it matters:**
Operating a food business without proper financial registration can result in fines or forced closure. This feature ensures stores can complete compliance steps directly from the platform without needing separate paperwork.

---

## 2.8 Syncing Data to Franchise Centre

**What it does:**
Pushes all of the store's current data up to the franchise or chain headquarters system.

**Who uses it:**
Individual store managers in a franchise network, typically at the end of a period or when directed by headquarters.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Ensure headquarters has up-to-date product, pricing, and operational data
- Synchronise after making local changes that need to be visible chain-wide

**Why it matters:**
In a franchise model, HQ needs visibility into what each store is doing. This sync keeps the chain's central view accurate without requiring manual data entry at headquarters.

---

## 2.9 Checking Store Permissions

**What it does:**
Retrieves the custom permission settings for the store, determining which features and modules are accessible.

**Who uses it:**
The system, automatically, to determine what a user can see and do when they log in.

**What they can accomplish:**
- The store automatically sees only the features their subscription or agreement includes
- Administrators understand what capabilities are enabled for their location

**Why it matters:**
Different stores on different subscription tiers should only access the features they've paid for. This prevents confusion and ensures fair access control.

---

## 2.10 Uploading Files & Images

**What it does:**
Allows users to upload images (product photos, share card posters, profile pictures) to the platform's storage.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers and marketing staff adding visual content to products or promotions.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Upload product photos for the menu
- Add promotional poster images for WeChat share cards
- Attach supporting images to any feature that accepts them

**Why it matters:**
Visual content dramatically improves the customer experience — a product with a photo sells better than one without. This feature makes it simple to keep visual content fresh and professional.

# SECTION 3 — MANAGING YOUR MENU
### (Products, Categories & Goods)

---

## 3.1 Browsing the Product Catalogue (SPU List)

**What it does:**
Shows a searchable, filterable list of all products (at the "product unit" level — e.g., "Kung Pao Chicken" regardless of size or variant).

**Who uses it:**
Store managers, menu designers, and purchasing staff reviewing what's on offer.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Browse the full product catalogue with search and filters
- Filter by category, brand, vendor, or status
- See which products are active vs. inactive
- Identify products not yet mapped to their store (for cloud goods sync)

**Why it matters:**
A restaurant can have hundreds of menu items. Being able to quickly find, filter, and review products is essential for menu management — especially when preparing for a seasonal menu change or a new promotional period.

---

## 3.2 Managing Product Variants & Stock Units (SKU List)

**What it does:**
Shows the specific variants of each product — different sizes, flavours, or preparation styles (e.g., "Kung Pao Chicken — Large", "Kung Pao Chicken — Small").

**Who uses it:**
Store managers and kitchen staff who need to manage pricing and stock at the variant level.

**What they can accomplish:**
- View all size/variant combinations for every product
- See different pricing tiers (wholesale price 1/2/3/4)
- Filter and search by product code or specification
- Select specific SKUs for use in promotions, purchase orders, or stock templates

**Why it matters:**
A single "product" on the menu might have dozens of variants with different costs and prices. Managing these correctly ensures accurate pricing, stock tracking, and promotion targeting.

---

## 3.3 Featured / Favourite Items

**What it does:**
Retrieves a pre-configured list of featured or favourite products associated with a specific "favourites" collection.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers curating highlighted menu items, or the smart ordering system pulling recommended items.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Pull a specific curated product list for promotional display
- Feed featured items into recommendation engines or digital menu boards

**Why it matters:**
Highlighting popular or high-margin items increases their sales. This feature powers "featured dishes" sections on digital menus or customer-facing ordering screens.

---

## 3.4 Organising Product Categories

**What it does:**
Provides the full category structure for products — both as a tree (for navigation) and as flat options (for dropdowns and pickers).

**Who uses it:**
Store managers setting up or reorganising their menu structure.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Browse the category hierarchy (e.g., Mains → Hot Dishes → Meat)
- Add products to the correct category
- Filter product lists by category
- Configure which categories appear in online ordering

**Why it matters:**
A well-organised menu is easier for customers to navigate and for staff to manage. Categories also drive how reports are structured — understanding sales "by category" requires clean categorisation.

---

## 3.5 Managing Brands

**What it does:**
Allows the store to maintain a list of product brands, and search/filter products by brand.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers in multi-brand restaurants or ghost kitchens operating several concepts.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Associate products with their brand
- Filter the product catalogue by brand
- Report on performance by brand

**Why it matters:**
In a multi-brand food hall or ghost kitchen, knowing which brand each product belongs to is essential for brand-specific reporting, pricing, and marketing.

---

## 3.6 Managing Vendors / Suppliers

**What it does:**
Maintains the list of food and goods suppliers the store purchases from.

**Who uses it:**
Purchasing staff and store managers when creating purchase orders or reviewing supplier relationships.

**What they can accomplish:**
- See all approved suppliers in one place
- Associate products with their source supplier
- Filter and select vendors when creating purchase orders
- Review vendor check/audit history

**Why it matters:**
Knowing exactly where each ingredient comes from is critical for food safety, cost control, and supplier negotiation. If a supplier raises prices or has a quality issue, the team can quickly identify which products are affected.

---

## 3.7 Nutrition Information

**What it does:**
Stores and retrieves nutritional information for menu items (calories, macros, allergens, etc.).

**Who uses it:**
Store managers and compliance staff maintaining menu transparency.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Record and update nutritional data for menu items
- Retrieve nutrition info for display on menus or customer-facing apps
- Meet regulatory requirements for nutrition labelling

**Why it matters:**
Increasingly, customers want to know what's in their food — and in many markets, disclosing nutritional information is legally required. Having this data in the system means it can be consistently published wherever needed.

---

## 3.8 Managing Dining Areas & Zones

**What it does:**
Allows the store to define and manage the physical zones or areas within the dining space (e.g., "Indoor Seating", "Outdoor Terrace", "Private Dining Room").

**Who uses it:**
Restaurant managers setting up table management or configuring delivery zones.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Create and name different seating areas
- Assign tables to specific zones
- Use zones to filter availability during busy periods

**Why it matters:**
For table service restaurants, knowing the layout — which tables are in which area — is fundamental to efficient seating, waitstaff assignment, and reservation management.

---

## 3.9 Managing Tables

**What it does:**
Maintains a complete list of tables in the restaurant, including their type, area, and capacity.

**Who uses it:**
Restaurant floor managers and front-of-house staff.

**What they can accomplish:**
- See all tables across all dining areas
- Filter tables by zone or table type
- Know table availability at a glance

**Why it matters:**
Table management is the heartbeat of a table-service restaurant. Knowing which tables are free, occupied, or reserved — and where they physically are — is essential for smooth service and maximising covers.

---

## 3.10 Warehouse Management

**What it does:**
Provides a list of warehouses associated with the store or chain for stock management purposes.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers and warehouse/stock staff.

**What they can accomplish:**
- View all warehouse locations
- Associate stock movements with specific warehouses
- Manage multi-location inventory

**Why it matters:**
Larger restaurant groups or central kitchens often have separate storage locations. Tracking which warehouse holds which stock prevents ordering items you already have and ensures stock is available where it's needed.

---

## 3.11 Batch Stock Management

**What it does:**
Allows stock to be managed and tracked in batches — groups of items that arrived together and share the same expiry date or lot number.

**Who uses it:**
Warehouse and kitchen staff managing perishable inventory.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Track stock arrivals as discrete batches
- Apply first-in-first-out (FIFO) logic to stock usage
- Know exactly when each batch expires

**Why it matters:**
Batch tracking is the gold standard for food safety and waste reduction. It ensures the oldest stock is used first, and gives full traceability if a food safety issue arises.

---

## 3.12 Stock Templates

**What it does:**
Provides pre-configured templates for common stock operations — making stock counts, adjustments, and orders faster to complete.

**Who uses it:**
Purchasing and warehouse staff doing routine stock work.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Use templates to quickly create standard purchase orders
- Apply reorder templates for frequently purchased items
- Reduce data entry for repetitive stock tasks

**Why it matters:**
A restaurant re-orders the same core ingredients every week. Templates eliminate repetitive data entry, reduce errors, and save staff significant time on routine tasks.

---

## 3.13 Checking Sub-Store Promotion Validity

**What it does:**
Validates whether a proposed promotion is compatible with sub-store configurations in the chain before it goes live.

**Who uses it:**
Franchise managers and marketing staff at headquarters rolling out chain-wide promotions.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Verify a promotion will work correctly across all participating locations
- Catch configuration conflicts before launch
- Avoid situations where some stores can't honour a chain-wide deal

**Why it matters:**
A chain-wide "20% off" promotion that fails at 3 out of 10 locations creates angry customers and embarrassed staff. Pre-flight validation prevents this.

---

## 3.14 Getting Parent Store for Purchase Orders

**What it does:**
Identifies the parent/headquarters store that a sub-store should raise purchase orders to.

**Who uses it:**
Sub-store purchasing staff in a franchise or central supply chain model.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Know exactly which central store to order from
- Ensure purchase orders are routed to the correct supplier entity within the chain

**Why it matters:**
In franchise models, sub-stores often buy from central kitchens or HQ warehouses rather than external suppliers. This ensures orders flow to the right place automatically.

# SECTION 4 — CUSTOMER MANAGEMENT
### (Loyalty, Grades, Labels, Points & Promotions)

---

## 4.1 Customer List & Search

**What it does:**
Provides a comprehensive, searchable list of all registered customers with powerful filtering options.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers, marketing staff, and customer service teams.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Search customers by name, membership grade, registration date, birthday, or WeChat status
- Filter by account balance, points balance, activity status, or label
- Identify customers meeting specific criteria for targeted campaigns
- Export or review customer data for analysis

**Why it matters:**
A customer database is only valuable if you can find the right customers quickly. Whether you're looking for "all Gold members who haven't visited in 60 days" or "customers with a balance over ¥500," precise filtering turns raw data into actionable segments.

---

## 4.2 Customer Dashboard Summary

**What it does:**
Shows at-a-glance metrics about the store's entire customer base.

**Who uses it:**
Store owners and managers reviewing the health of their loyalty programme.

**What they can accomplish:**
- See total number of registered customers
- Check total outstanding account balances (stored value / charge accounts)
- View total accumulated loyalty points across all customers
- Track total recharge/top-up amounts

**Why it matters:**
These four numbers tell a manager the overall health of their customer relationship programme in seconds. Rising customer numbers and balances = a healthy, engaged customer base. Declining metrics = time to run a re-engagement campaign.

---

## 4.3 Customer Grades / Tiers

**What it does:**
Manages the loyalty tier structure — Bronze, Silver, Gold, VIP, or whatever tiers the store defines.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers designing or reviewing their loyalty programme structure.

**What they can accomplish:**
- View all tier levels and their names
- Assign customers to specific tiers
- Batch-import grade assignments for large customer groups
- Use grades to target promotions to specific loyalty levels

**Why it matters:**
Tiered loyalty programmes are proven to drive spending — customers work toward the next tier. Having clear, manageable tiers with distinct benefits gives customers a reason to keep coming back and spend more.

---

## 4.4 Customer Labels / Tags

**What it does:**
Allows stores to create and apply custom tags to customer records.

**Who uses it:**
Marketing staff and store managers segmenting their customer base.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Tag customers with meaningful labels (e.g., "Birthday This Month", "Lapsed Customer", "Corporate Account", "Allergy: Nuts")
- Use labels as filters when selecting promotion targets
- Build nuanced customer segments beyond what grades alone provide

**Why it matters:**
Grades tell you how loyal a customer is. Labels tell you *who they are*. A customer who is both "Gold member" and "Birthday this month" is a perfect target for a birthday reward campaign. Labels make personalisation possible at scale.

---

## 4.5 Customer Attributes & Specs

**What it does:**
Retrieves configurable customer attribute options — custom fields that can be attached to customer profiles.

**Who uses it:**
Store administrators configuring what additional information is captured for customers.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Define custom data fields for customer profiles
- Capture store-specific information (e.g., preferred table, corporate employer, dietary preferences)
- Use custom attributes for filtering and segmentation

**Why it matters:**
Every business has unique information they want to know about their customers. Custom attributes let the system adapt to the store's specific needs rather than forcing everyone into the same rigid data model.

---

## 4.6 Manually Adjusting Customer Points

**What it does:**
Allows authorised staff to manually add or deduct loyalty points from a customer's account.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers and customer service staff resolving point-related issues.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Award bonus points as compensation for a poor experience
- Correct points that were missed during a transaction
- Deduct points used for a reward that was processed incorrectly
- Handle goodwill gestures for loyal customers

**Why it matters:**
No automated system is perfect. There will always be cases where a customer's points didn't record correctly or where a manager wants to reward exceptional loyalty with a bonus. Manual adjustment is the safety valve that keeps customers happy and trust in the programme intact.

---

## 4.7 Resetting a Customer's Password

**What it does:**
Allows store staff to reset the password of a customer's membership account.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers or customer service staff when a member is locked out.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Help a customer regain access to their loyalty account
- Reset passwords at the counter without the customer needing to use self-service recovery

**Why it matters:**
Customers who can't access their points or membership card get frustrated at the point of sale. Being able to reset instantly from the counter prevents a loyalty programme from creating friction instead of goodwill.

---

## 4.8 Removing a Customer Record

**What it does:**
Permanently deletes a customer profile from the store's records.

**Who uses it:**
Store administrators handling data privacy requests or cleaning up records.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Honour customer requests to be forgotten (GDPR or local data privacy compliance)
- Remove duplicate or test accounts from the system

**Why it matters:**
Data privacy regulations in many countries require businesses to delete customer data upon request. Having this capability built in means the store can comply quickly and confidently.

---

## 4.9 Batch Import Customer Grades

**What it does:**
Allows the store to upload and assign loyalty grades to multiple customers at once.

**Who uses it:**
Store managers when launching a new loyalty programme or migrating from another system.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Assign grades to hundreds or thousands of customers at once
- Migrate existing tier status when switching loyalty systems
- Quickly restructure customer tiers after a programme redesign

**Why it matters:**
Manually assigning grades one customer at a time for a database of 10,000 members is not realistic. Batch import makes large-scale programme launches or restructures feasible in hours instead of weeks.

---

## 4.10 Customer Promotions & Campaigns

**What it does:**
The full campaign creation and management system — allowing stores to create targeted promotional campaigns with complex eligibility rules, timing, and rewards.

**Who uses it:**
Marketing staff and store managers designing customer engagement initiatives.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Create promotions with precise date and time windows (including blackout dates, specific days of the week, or specific monthly dates)
- Target specific customer groups (all customers, specific grades, specific individuals, or WeChat members only)
- Set stock limits (total campaign budget, per-customer limits, per-day limits)
- Send SMS or WeChat notifications to eligible customers
- Enable social sharing of the promotion
- Attach a custom share poster image, WeChat Mini Program link, or H5 page URL
- Apply promotions across multiple store locations simultaneously
- Set holiday adjustment rules (pause during public holidays)
- Edit existing promotions or view them in read-only mode

**Why it matters:**
Promotions are the engine of customer retention. The difference between a promotion that works and one that doesn't often comes down to targeting and timing. A well-configured campaign that reaches the right people at the right time — with automatic notifications and social sharing built in — can dramatically drive repeat visits and revenue.

---

## 4.11 Available Coupons List

**What it does:**
Shows all coupons that are currently available for customers to collect and redeem.

**Who uses it:**
Customer-facing screens, marketing staff reviewing active offers, and promotion configuration tools.

**What they can accomplish:**
- See all currently active and collectable coupons
- Filter by shareable status or redemption scope
- Include coupon rewards in promotion campaigns

**Why it matters:**
Coupons are one of the most popular and effective customer loyalty tools in food service. Having a live, accurate view of what's available ensures customers always see valid offers, and marketing staff can see what's in play before launching new campaigns.

---

## 4.12 Points Exchange Rules

**What it does:**
Defines the rules for how customers can exchange loyalty points for rewards (coupons, dishes, or discounts).

**Who uses it:**
Store managers designing or updating the loyalty rewards catalogue.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Set how many points are needed to redeem a specific reward
- Define which coupons or dishes can be exchanged for points
- Set limits on how many redemptions are allowed (total, per person, per day)
- Configure the validity period for exchange rules
- Check for duplicate rules before saving (preventing conflicting point values)

**Why it matters:**
A loyalty programme where customers earn points but can never use them in a way they find valuable quickly becomes ignored. Clear, generous, and well-managed exchange rules are what make customers actually care about accumulating points.

# SECTION 5 — SALES & PERFORMANCE
### (Dashboard & Reporting)

---

## 5.1 Sales Dashboard

**What it does:**
Loads a complete summary of the store's sales performance — key metrics, weekly breakdown, and trend data — all in one view.

**Who uses it:**
Store owners and managers checking business performance, typically first thing in the morning or at end of day.

**What they can accomplish:**
- See today's revenue, order count, and other KPIs at a glance
- Compare performance against previous periods
- View weekly patterns (which days are busiest)
- Spot trends over time (is business growing or declining?)
- See a breakdown by payment method

**Why it matters:**
Running a restaurant without knowing your numbers is flying blind. The dashboard gives owners and managers the information they need to make decisions — whether to run a quiet-day promotion, call in extra staff for a predicted busy period, or investigate why sales have dropped. Getting this data in seconds rather than pulling manual reports saves hours each week.

The smart traffic system automates this at scale.

# SECTION 7 — PAYMENTS
### (AiBao Pay & Payment Services)

---

## 7.1 Payment Service Configuration

**What it does:**
Retrieves the configuration details for the store's payment service proxy — the connection between the store and its payment processing infrastructure.

**Who uses it:**
Store administrators during payment setup, or the system automatically when initialising payment terminals.

**What they can accomplish:**
- Confirm which payment gateway the store is connected to
- Verify internal account linkage is correct
- Troubleshoot payment configuration issues

**Why it matters:**
If payments don't work, the restaurant can't take orders. Having clear visibility into the payment service configuration means issues can be identified and resolved quickly, minimising downtime.
